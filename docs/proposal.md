## Reflection

This posting is directly relevant to this analytics engineering course because the Analyst, Pricing Strategy role at Visa centers on the same skills we have been building all semester: querying and synthesizing large datasets, building rigorous quantitative analyses, and turning raw data into actionable recommendations for business leaders. The role requires financial modeling and data synthesis across complex business problems — tasks where SQL, dimensional modeling, and a well-structured data pipeline are the foundational tools. To prove I can do this work, I would build an end-to-end pipeline that pulls Visa's publicly available transaction and payment network data from a financial API, loads it into Snowflake, transforms it through dbt staging and mart layers into a star schema organized around pricing metrics, and surfaces it through a Streamlit dashboard that answers questions like how merchant acceptance rates and interchange volumes shift across regions and deal types. This same project transfers cleanly to two or three adjacent roles: a Business Intelligence Analyst at a fintech company like PayPal or Stripe, where the analytical focus is also on transaction pricing and revenue optimization, or a Revenue Analytics Analyst at any financial services firm that needs SQL-driven pipeline infrastructure to support strategic pricing decisions.
